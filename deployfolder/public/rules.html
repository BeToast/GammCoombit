The Rules Page

   Welcome, the rules are as follows:
   There are two ways to win...
    1. move your King to the opponents corner.
    2. checkmate the enemy king.
   Moving your king to the opposite corner of the board is much easier than checkmating the enemy king.

   What is check and checkmate?
    check : when a piece is moved and a king is put underattack as a result of the moved piece.
    what happens when I'm in check??
     If a king is 'in check' the king must move to a safe space
     Or an ally piece must capture the piece that is threatening the king
    checkmate : when there is no move to escape being in check. If you are in checkmate you have lost.

   What is a move?
    your turn consists of moving a piece
    where a piece can move is shown by green circles when dragging a piece
    all pieces can only move 1 space at a time.
What is a capture?
    capture : when an ally piece moves and takes the place of a enemy piece.
    lets take a look at how pieces can capture and move.

This is the rook
 the rook can move horozontally and vertically
 the rook can capture horozontally and vertically

Note: The arrows on the piece is where the piece can capture

This is the king
 the king can move any direction
 the king cannot move into check, unless it is the opponents corner.
 the king can capture any direction
 the king cannot capture a piece that puts him in check

This is the bishop
 the bishop can move any direction
 the bishop can only capture diagonally

Here, the bishop cannot capture the enemy king as the bishop only captues diagonally.
If any of this is confusing dont worry, you can see your opotions for each piece by dragging it ingame.
